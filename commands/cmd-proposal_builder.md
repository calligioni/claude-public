## PHASE 1 — PROPOSAL STRATEGY

Before writing, identify:

- The single most emotional pain they described on the call — this becomes the opening
- The specific outcome they said they want — this becomes the promise
- The objection most likely to kill the deal — this gets addressed inside the proposal, not in a footnote
- The right price framing: anchor high, or justify value first?

Output a one-paragraph **Proposal Strategy Note** for your own reference.

---

## PHASE 2 — WRITE THE FULL PROPOSAL

Write a complete client-facing proposal with these sections in order:

**THE SITUATION**
- 2–3 sentences that describe their current situation back to them with precision
- Use their exact words where you remember them
- Do not mention your company here — this section is entirely about them
- They should read this and think "they actually listened"

**THE COST OF STAYING THE SAME**
- One short paragraph on what it costs them (in money, time, or missed opportunity) to do nothing
- Be specific — use numbers they gave you on the call, or reasonable estimates based on their industry
- Not dramatic. Matter-of-fact. The facts do the work.

**WHAT WE'LL BUILD**
- Clear description of the deliverable: what the AI system does, how it works in their business
- Written in plain English — no technical jargon unless they used it first
- Focus on what it does for them, not how it works
- 3–5 bullet points maximum

**HOW IT WORKS**
- Simple 3-step process: Discovery, Build, Launch
- Each step: one line on what happens, one line on what they need to do
- Timeline for each step

**THE INVESTMENT**
- Present the price clearly — no small print, no ambiguity
- If offering tiers, offer two options max: a core option and a premium option
- Frame price relative to what they told you they're losing or spending now
- Payment terms on the same line as the price

**WHAT HAPPENS NEXT**
- One clear action for them to take: sign, reply, or call
- A specific deadline that creates urgency without being pushy (e.g. "I can start your build the week of [date] if we confirm this week")
- Do not end with "let me know if you have any questions" — it invites delay

---

## PHASE 3 — OBJECTION INOCULATION

Pick the single most likely objection from your call notes. Write one paragraph inside the proposal that addresses it proactively — before they raise it. Place it naturally within the relevant section, not as a standalone FAQ.

---

## RULES

- No agency buzzwords: "bespoke", "cutting-edge", "seamlessly", "robust", "leverage"
- Write in second person — "you" and "your business", not "the client"
- Every number must be specific — never "significant savings", always "X hours per week" or "X leads per month"
- The proposal should be readable in under 4 minutes
- Do not include a company bio, credentials section, or case studies list — those belong in the pitch deck, not the proposal
