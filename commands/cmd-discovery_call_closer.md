## PHASE 2 — THE CALL FRAMEWORK

Write a full discovery call framework with spoken dialogue examples for each section.

**OPENING (2 minutes)**
- Warm but efficient. Set the agenda so they know how the call will run.
- Ask one genuine question about something specific in their business before going into discovery.
- Never start with "so tell me about your business" — it signals you didn't do your homework.

**DISCOVERY (10–15 minutes)**
Write 8–10 questions in the order they should be asked. Structure them to move from:
- Current situation (what does today look like?)
- Pain (what's not working?)
- Impact (what does that cost you in time, money, or stress?)
- Previous attempts (what have you tried?)
- Desired outcome (what does good look like?)

For each question, write:
- The question itself
- Why you're asking it (internal note in brackets)
- A follow-up probe if they give a surface-level answer

**THE PIVOT (2 minutes)**
- Summarise back what they told you in their own words
- Ask one confirmation question: "Is that a fair summary?"
- Transition into the solution only after they confirm

**THE PITCH (5 minutes)**
- Present only what is relevant to the specific pains they mentioned
- Use their exact words back at them
- Show one result from a similar client — specific, not vague
- Never list features. Only describe outcomes.

**THE CLOSE (3 minutes)**
- Ask a direct closing question — do not trail off
- Give two clear options for next steps, not an open-ended "what do you think?"
- If they need to think about it: set a specific follow-up time before you hang up. Never leave it open.

## PHASE 3 — OBJECTION HANDLERS

Write a handler for each of the 3 objections identified in Phase 1, plus these three universal ones:

- "It's too expensive"
- "I need to think about it"
- "Can you send me more information?"

Each handler: acknowledge, reframe, ask one question. Max 4 lines.

## PHASE 4 — POST-CALL ACTION

Output a post-call checklist:
- What to send within 1 hour of the call ending
- What to say in the follow-up message
- When to follow up if they go quiet

## RULES

- Every question should feel like genuine curiosity, not an interrogation
- Never interrupt — write [pause and let them finish] at key moments
- If they ask about price before you've established value: "I want to make sure I quote you accurately — can I ask a couple more questions first?"
- The call ends with a clear next step or it didn't happen
