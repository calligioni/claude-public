## PHASE 1 — PRE-CALL INTELLIGENCE

Based on the prospect details above, identify:

- The single most likely operational problem their business has right now
- What language their industry uses to describe that problem (use their words, not yours)
- The best hook angle: time wasted, leads slipping, staff costs, or missed revenue
- Any local or industry-specific detail you can drop to prove you know their world

Output a one-paragraph **Pre-Call Brief** before writing the script.

---

## PHASE 2 — WRITE THE FULL CALL SCRIPT

Structure the script in clearly labelled sections. Write every line as spoken dialogue — natural rhythm, short sentences, room to breathe.

**OPENER (first 8 seconds)**
- State your name and why you're calling in one breath
- No "how are you today" — it signals telemarketer instantly
- Immediate relevance hook: reference something specific about their business

**PERMISSION BRIDGE (5 seconds)**
- Ask for 30 seconds, not a meeting
- Frame it as "I noticed something" not "I have something to sell you"

**THE HOOK (20–30 seconds)**
- Describe the problem in their language before you mention any solution
- Make them feel seen — not sold to
- One specific stat or result from a similar business if possible

**THE PIVOT (10 seconds)**
- Transition from problem to possibility without pitching the service yet
- Ask a diagnostic question that they will almost certainly say yes to

**THE ASK (10 seconds)**
- Ask for a short call this week — not "whenever works for you"
- Give two specific time options
- If they say yes: confirm, thank them briefly, hang up. Do not keep talking.

**OBJECTION HANDLERS**

Write a response for each of these four objections:
1. "I'm not interested"
2. "We already have something like that"
3. "Send me an email"
4. "I don't have time right now"

Each handler: 2–3 lines max. Calm, not pushy. Acknowledge first, redirect second, ask one small question third.

**THE VOICEMAIL (if no answer)**
- 20–25 seconds when spoken aloud
- Specific enough that they know you're not mass-dialling
- End with your number said slowly, twice

---

## RULES

- Write in spoken English — read it aloud and it should sound natural
- No jargon, no "AI-powered", no "cutting-edge solution"
- Never ask "is this a good time" — it invites a no
- Pause markers: write [pause] where a natural beat should happen
- Tone: confident, calm, brief. Like someone who doesn't need the sale.

---
