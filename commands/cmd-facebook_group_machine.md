## PHASE 1 — GROUP IDENTIFICATION

Identify the best Facebook Groups where your target clients spend time. Think in three categories:

**Industry groups** — groups your prospects run or join to talk about their business problems
**Peer groups** — groups where your niche hangs out to share wins, ask questions, and vent
**Local groups** — city or region-specific business groups where local owners congregate

For each category, output:
- 5 specific group name suggestions (real naming patterns, not made up)
- What type of posts perform best in that group
- The tone of the community (formal, casual, supportive, competitive)
- The right reason for you to be in that group that doesn't feel like infiltration

---

## PHASE 2 — THE COMMENT STRATEGY

The goal of commenting is to be the most helpful person in the room consistently, so that when you eventually DM someone, they already know you.

Write a comment strategy with:

**Trigger posts to look for:**
- 5 specific types of posts that signal a prospect is ready to hear about automation (complaints, questions, milestone posts, hiring posts, frustration posts)
- Example of what each trigger post might actually say

**Comment frameworks:**
Write 3 comment templates for each trigger type above. Each comment must:
- Add genuine value or insight first
- Never mention your service in the comment
- End in a way that opens a natural conversation
- Sound like it came from someone experienced, not someone prospecting
- Be 3–5 lines max

---

## PHASE 3 — THE DM AFTER THE COMMENT

Once someone engages with your comment or you've commented on their post, write the follow-up DM.

**Hard constraints:**
- 70–90 characters. Count them.
- One or two sentences. No emojis. No em dashes. No en dashes.
- References the specific post or comment thread — makes it feel personal, not copy-pasted.
- Lowercase throughout.

**Format:**
```
[reference to their post or your exchange]. [what you noticed or made, worth a look.]
```

**Example:**
- `saw your post about losing leads on weekends. think i know the fix.` (68 chars)

---

## PHASE 4 — THE 30-DAY ACTIVITY PLAN

Output a simple week-by-week plan:

**Week 1:** Join and observe. Do not post or comment yet. Note the top contributors and most common pain points.
**Week 2:** Start commenting on 3–5 posts per day using the frameworks above. No DMs yet.
**Week 3:** DM anyone who engaged positively with your comments. Continue commenting daily.
**Week 4:** Post one value-led piece of content in each group. No pitch. Pure insight.

For each week, output one example action in specific detail.

---

## RULES

- Never post your service in a group unless the group explicitly allows promo
- Never comment and DM the same person on the same day
- If a group has a weekly promo thread, use it sparingly and make it about results not features
- Consistency beats volume — 5 genuine comments a day outperforms 50 generic ones
